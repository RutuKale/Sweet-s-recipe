Gulab Jamun

Ingredients:

  1. Milk powder or Khoya
  2. All-purpose flour (Maida)
  3. Baking powder
  4. Butter or Ghee
  5. Milk

Recipe:

  1. Make the Dough: Combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and ghee. Gradually add milk to form a smooth dough.
  2. Shape the Dough: Roll the dough into small, smooth balls.
  3. Prepare the Syrup: In a pan, dissolve sugar in water over medium heat. Add cardamom powder and rose water or saffron if using. Let it simmer until it becomes a sticky syrup.
  4. Fry the Dough Balls: Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the dough balls until they are golden brown.
  5. Soak in Syrup: Place the fried balls in the warm syrup and let them soak for at least 30 minutes.

Rabri

Ingredients:

  1. Whole Milk
  2. Sugar
  3. Cardamom Powder
  4. Saffron Strands
  5. Chopped Nuts
  6. Rose Water or Kewra Water

Recipe:

  1. Boil the Milk: In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the whole milk to a boil.
  2. Reduce the Milk: Continuously simmer and stir the milk, allowing it to reduce and thicken. Scrape the sides of the pan to incorporate the malai (cream) back into the milk.
  3. Add Sugar and Flavorings: Once the milk has thickened to your desired consistency, add sugar, cardamom powder, saffron strands, and rose or kewra water if using. Stir well to combine.
  4. Cool and Garnish: Allow the mixture to cool, then garnish with chopped nuts before serving.

Roshogolla

Ingredients:

  1. Milk
  2. Lemon Juice or Vinegar
  3. Water
  4. Sugar
  5. Cardamom
  6. Rose Water or Kewra Water (optional)

Recipe:

  1. Making chhena
    • Boil the milk and add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it.
    • Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth to separate the chhena.
    • Rinse the chhena under cold water to remove the lemon or vinegar taste, then squeeze out excess water and hang it for about 30 minutes to remove any remaining moisture.
  2. Kneading Chhena:
    • Knead the chhena until it becomes smooth and soft. This step is crucial for ensuring soft and spongy Rasgullas.
    • Divide the kneaded chhena into small, smooth balls without any cracks.
  3. Preparing Sugar Syrup:
    • In a large pot, combine water and sugar. Bring it to a boil to create a thin sugar syrup.
    • Optionally, add cardamom or rose water/kewra water for additional flavor.
  4. Cooking Rasgullas:
    • Add the chhena balls to the boiling sugar syrup.
    • Cover the pot and cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes. The Rasgullas will double in size as they cook.
    • Ensure that the syrup remains boiling throughout the cooking process. If it thickens too much, add a little more water.
  5. Cooling and Serving:
    • Once cooked, let the Rasgullas cool in the syrup.
    • Serve chilled or at room temperature.
